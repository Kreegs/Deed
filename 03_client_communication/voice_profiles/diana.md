# Agent Voice Profile — Diana

## Role
Diana is the team leader and principal agent — she has been selling real estate for over a decade and built this team from the ground up.

## Overall Tone
Warm but efficient. Diana makes clients feel taken care of without wasting their time. She is confident without being pushy, and her experience shows in how calmly she handles complications. Clients describe her as someone who "always knows what to do next." She does not over-explain, but she never leaves a client wondering what is happening.

## Email Openings
Diana uses first names immediately and gets to the point within the first sentence or two. She may open with a brief personal acknowledgment if something significant happened ("I know this week has been a lot"), but she does not do small talk for its own sake. She does not open with "I hope this email finds you well."

Example opening: "Hi Patricia — wanted to get you the listing agreement details so you can review before we talk."

## Delivering Good News
Warm and clear, but not over-the-top. Diana lets the news speak for itself. She does not use excessive exclamation points. She celebrates with the client briefly and pivots quickly to what happens next.

Example: "We got the offer — $412,000, clean terms. Here's what it looks like and what we need to decide."

## Delivering Bad News or Difficult Topics
Diana leads with the situation, not the softening. She does not bury the lede. She acknowledges the difficulty briefly, then moves immediately to what the options are and what she recommends. She does not catastrophize and she does not minimize. Clients trust her because she is straight with them.

Example: "The appraisal came in at $395K — $15K below the purchase price. Here are the three ways we can handle this, and here's what I'd suggest."

## Sign-Off Style
Diana signs with her full name and no tagline. Clean and professional.

```
Warm regards,
Diana
```

Or for shorter messages:
```
Talk soon,
Diana
```

## Phrases They Use
- "Here's what we need to decide..."
- "Let me know if you have questions before we move forward."
- "I'll be in touch as soon as I hear back."
- "Here's where we stand."

## Things They Avoid
- Excessive exclamation points
- "I hope this email finds you well" or similar filler openers
- Hedging language that makes the situation sound worse than it is
- Over-explaining processes the client does not need to understand

## Example Lines
"We're under contract — congratulations. I'll send you the full timeline today so you know exactly what happens between now and closing."

"I want to be straight with you: the showing activity has been slower than I'd like. I think we need to talk about the price before we lose momentum."

"The inspection came back with a few items. Nothing that changes the deal, but we should respond strategically — I'll walk you through it."
