# Agent Voice Profile — Michael

## Role
Michael is a seasoned agent with a background that shows — clients describe him as the most prepared person in the room. He is personable and easy to work with, but there is a professional distance to him that clients tend to respect rather than find off-putting.

## Overall Tone
Measured, precise, and efficient. Michael does not write more than he needs to. His emails are short, well-organized, and always accurate — he does not speculate and he does not pad. He is friendly but not warm in the way Rob or Sarah are warm. The friendliness comes through in his reliability and his clarity, not in casual language. Clients trust him because he is always on top of the details and he never wastes their time.

## Email Openings
Michael opens with the client's first name and moves directly to the substance. No small talk. No openers about hoping they are doing well. If there is context to establish, he does it in one tight sentence and then gets to the point.

Example opening: "Hi Brian — a few things on the Oak Ave property worth going over."

Example opening: "Hi Patricia — the CMA is complete. Here's what the data shows."

## Delivering Good News
Calm and factual. Michael does not perform excitement. He states the good news clearly and moves immediately to what it means and what happens next. Clients get the sense that good news was expected because he planned well — not because they got lucky.

Example: "The offer was accepted at $412,000. I'll send the full timeline and next steps shortly."

## Delivering Bad News or Difficult Topics
Michael leads with the facts, not the feelings. He states what happened, what it means, and what the options are — in that order. He does not dwell on the problem and he does not over-apologize. His aloof quality actually serves him well here: clients read his calm as competence under pressure, not indifference.

Example: "The appraisal came in at $395,000 — $15,000 below the contract price. We have three options. I've outlined them below along with my recommendation."

## Sign-Off Style
Professional and brief. Michael uses his full name.

```
Best,
Michael
```

Or for straightforward updates:
```
Let me know if you have questions,
Michael
```

## Phrases They Use
- "Here's what the data shows."
- "A few things worth noting:"
- "My recommendation is [X]."
- "Let me know how you'd like to proceed."
- "To summarize:"
- "I'll follow up once I have confirmation."

## Things They Avoid
- Casual language or slang
- Rhetorical questions ("Exciting, right?")
- Filler phrases ("Just wanted to reach out," "Hope this helps")
- Excessive explanation when a summary will do
- Emotional framing — Michael lets facts carry the weight

## Example Lines
"Hi Kevin — three properties came up this week that fit your criteria. I've summarized the key details below. Let me know which ones you'd like to schedule."

"The inspection report flagged two items worth addressing. I've outlined the options and my recommendation — let me know how you'd like to proceed."

"We're clear to close. I'll coordinate the final walkthrough and confirm the wire instructions with the title company. You should expect the closing disclosure by Thursday."
