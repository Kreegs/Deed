# Template: First Contact

## When to Use
First outreach to a new buyer or seller lead. Triggered after qualification is complete and property research (or CMA) is done. This is the first message the client receives from the agent.

## Context to Read
- `lead_source` — determines opening warmth and whether to reference a referral name
- `referral_from` — if lead_source is "referral", open by mentioning the person who sent them
- `intent` — buy or sell shapes the entire message
- `contact_preference` — email / text / phone; note at top of draft if not email
- `budget_range` / `asking_price_target` — referenced only if directly relevant
- Property report or CMA — summarize one key finding to show you have done your homework
- `timeline` — reference if it creates urgency

## Subject Line
- Buyer: `[Property address] — [one-line hook, e.g., "Let's Talk" or "Worth a Showing"]`
- Seller: `Your [street name] Listing — Next Steps`
- Referral (either): `[Referral person's name] suggested I reach out`

## Required Content (in order)

1. **Opening acknowledgment** — how the lead came in, or who sent them. One sentence. Referrals get a warmer open than cold Zillow leads.

2. **Show your homework** — one specific detail that proves you have looked at their situation. For buyers: something from the property report or their criteria. For sellers: one finding from the CMA. Do not dump the full report. One concrete thing.

3. **Offer the next step** — what you are proposing. For buyers: a showing, a shortlist, a call. For sellers: a listing conversation, a walkthrough, a call to review the CMA. Make it easy to say yes — offer a specific time or ask what works.

4. **Close** — short and direct. No padding.

## Tone Notes
- Match warmth to lead source. Referrals feel warmer than cold inquiries.
- Do not oversell. The goal is to start a conversation, not close the deal in one email.
- Short is almost always better. If it feels long, cut it.

## Do Not Include
- Full property report or CMA details — one finding only
- Lengthy explanation of the team's background or credentials
- Multiple calls to action — one next step only
- Generic opener ("I wanted to reach out because...")
