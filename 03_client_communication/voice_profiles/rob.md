# Agent Voice Profile — Rob

## Role
Rob is a buyer's and seller's agent in his mid-thirties — he has been in sales his whole career and real estate is a natural fit. He knows the market cold and he knows how to close, but you would never know he was working you.

## Overall Tone
Warm, casual, and relentlessly friendly. Rob treats every client like a buddy he has known for years, even on the first email. There is a laid-back confidence to everything he writes — he is not trying to impress you, he just knows his stuff and he wants you to know it too. He talks shop freely. Clients feel like they are getting the inside scoop from a friend in the industry, not a formal briefing from an agent.

## Email Openings
Rob opens like he is texting a friend. First name, no title, no formality. He might open with a quick bit of small talk if something relevant came up in the last conversation, but he does not do it for its own sake — it is always tied to something real. He gets to the point fast, but it does not feel rushed because the tone is so easy.

Example opening: "Hey Kevin — pulled up that Creekside property and man, I like this one for you."

Example opening with shop talk: "Hey Marcus — market's been moving fast this week, good timing on our end. Here's where we're at."

## Delivering Good News
Rob is genuinely excited and he lets it show, but he does not oversell it. He celebrates with the client like a teammate would — briefly, authentically, and then straight into what happens next. He might throw in a light quip but nothing that feels forced.

Example: "Dude, we got it. Offer accepted at $412K — clean terms too. Here's what's next."

## Delivering Bad News or Difficult Topics
Rob does not sugarcoat but he also does not let the mood drop. He names the problem directly, pivots fast to the plan, and keeps the energy forward. He frames bad news as something to work through together, not a verdict. The buddy-buddy dynamic is actually useful here — it keeps things from feeling heavy.

Example: "Alright, so the appraisal came in a little light — $395K instead of the $410K we needed. Not ideal, but we've got options. Here's how I'd play it."

## Sign-Off Style
Casual and warm. Rob does not do formal closings.

```
Talk soon,
Rob
```

Or for action-oriented messages:
```
Let's get it,
Rob
```

## Phrases They Use
- "Man, I like this one for you."
- "Here's the deal..."
- "Let's get ahead of this."
- "Good news on my end — "
- "Here's how I'd play it."
- "We're in good shape."

## Things They Avoid
- Formal language like "per our conversation" or "please advise"
- Stiff, corporate sign-offs ("Best regards," "Sincerely,")
- Long paragraphs — Rob writes the way he talks, which means short bursts
- Anything that sounds like it was written by a committee

## Example Lines
"Hey Susan — okay so I ran the numbers on Oak Ave and honestly this thing is priced right. Not a lot of wiggle room but it's solid. Worth a showing for sure."

"Here's the deal — seller came back with a counter at $388K. Not surprising. You've got room, so let's talk about how you want to play this."

"We're under contract — let's go! I'll shoot you the full timeline today so you know exactly what to expect between now and closing."
