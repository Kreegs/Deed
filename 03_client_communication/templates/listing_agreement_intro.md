# Template: Listing Agreement Intro

## When to Use
Seller-only. Sent at `listing_prep` stage when the seller is ready to sign the listing agreement. Covers what the agreement says, what the seller is committing to, and what happens after they sign. Typically sent alongside or just before the DocuSign link.

## Context to Read
- `address_of_interest` — the property being listed
- `asking_price_target` — the agreed list price (may have been adjusted after CMA)
- `timeline` — the seller's stated timeframe to list and close
- `assigned_agent` — who is managing the listing
- `constraints.tags` — any constraints that affect the listing terms (e.g., `must_sell_first` affects contingency preferences; `estate_sale` means Patricia may need extra time or legal sign-off)
- `contact_log` — any context from the pricing conversation that should be acknowledged

## Subject Line
`Listing Agreement — [Street Address]`

## Required Content (in order)

1. **Brief personal note** (if appropriate) — one sentence acknowledging where the conversation has been. "It was great walking through the house with you" or "Glad we landed on a price that makes sense." Grounded in the actual prior interaction from the contact_log, not generic.

2. **What the agreement covers** — plain language, not legal. Key points: duration of the listing (e.g., 90 days), the agreed list price, that the team has exclusive right to sell, and the commission structure (agent knows this — reference it as "the commission we discussed" rather than stating terms that may vary).

3. **What happens after signing** — the immediate next steps in sequence:
   - Professional photos scheduled
   - Listing description written
   - Property listed on MLS
   - Seller reviews listing before it goes live
   Give a rough timeline if possible ("typically within the week").

4. **What to expect during the active listing** — brief. Showing process, how feedback is communicated, frequency of updates from the agent.

5. **Constraint acknowledgment** (if applicable) — if `estate_sale` or `divorce_sale` tags are present, acknowledge briefly that the agent knows additional coordination may be needed and will be in close contact.

6. **Call to action** — DocuSign link is being sent (or has been sent). Ask the seller to sign and reach out with any questions before doing so.

## Tone Notes
- Confident and organized. The seller should feel like they are in good hands with a team that has done this many times.
- Plain language throughout. Legal jargon does not belong here.
- Keep it practical. The seller does not need to understand everything about the listing agreement — they need to know the key terms and what happens next.

## Do Not Include
- Full legal terms or contingency language from the agreement
- Specific commission percentages or dollar amounts (reference "as discussed")
- Anything that sounds like you are still selling them on listing with you — the decision is made
