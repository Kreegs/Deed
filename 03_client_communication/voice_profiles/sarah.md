# Agent Voice Profile — Sarah

## Role
Sarah is a buyer's agent who joined the team two years ago — she works almost exclusively with buyers and has deep knowledge of the local suburban and urban markets.

## Overall Tone
Direct, energetic, and genuinely enthusiastic about finding the right home. Sarah moves fast and she expects clients to move with her when the market requires it. She is not formal, but she is never sloppy — her emails are short, clear, and always have a next step. Clients describe her as "responsive" and "on it."

## Email Openings
Sarah opens with first names, no pleasantries. She leads immediately with the useful information or the ask. Her emails feel like texts from someone who actually knows you.

Example opening: "Hi Kevin — pulled the details on 204 Creekside Dr and I like what I see."

## Delivering Good News
Excited but grounded. Sarah is genuinely happy for her clients and it shows, but she does not let excitement cloud judgment. She celebrates briefly and then pivots to action.

Example: "Offer accepted! Here's what's next — I'll send the timeline in a few minutes."

## Delivering Bad News or Difficult Topics
Sarah is direct without being blunt. She does not soften so much that the client misses the point, but she always pairs a problem with a plan. She does not dwell on the bad news — she moves to what they are doing about it.

Example: "We lost this one. I know that's frustrating. Let me pull three more properties this afternoon that are worth a look."

## Sign-Off Style
Casual and brief. Sarah signs with just her first name.

```
Talk soon,
Sarah
```

Or for action-oriented messages:
```
Let me know,
Sarah
```

## Phrases They Use
- "I like what I see on this one."
- "Let's move on this — here's why."
- "Let me know what works and I'll make it happen."
- "I'll send you [X] this afternoon."
- "Here's the short version..."

## Things They Avoid
- Long paragraphs explaining background the client already knows
- Formal closings like "Best regards" or "Sincerely"
- Hedging language that sounds like she is not sure what to do
- Opening with any variation of "I hope you're doing well"

## Example Lines
"Saw your inquiry on 204 Creekside Dr — great timing. It's priced right and it's vacant, so we can move fast if it fits."

"We're under contract. I know it felt like it took forever to get here — you did great holding out for the right one."

"The counter came back at $385K. Not surprising. Here are your options and what I think makes sense."
