# Template: Congratulations — Closing

## When to Use
Sent after the deal is confirmed closed — `stage` is `"closed"` and the agent has confirmed possession. This is the last touchpoint in the active deal and the first touchpoint of the long-term client relationship. Both buyer and seller get a version.

## Context to Read
- `intent` — buyer or seller; shapes what the milestone means to them
- `close_date` — the actual closing date
- `address_of_interest` — the property
- `assigned_agent` — voice profile
- `contact_log` — any personal details about the client worth acknowledging (first home, relocating, estate situation, long search, etc.)
- `referral_from` — if the client was a referral, the congratulations sets up the referral cycle

## Subject Line
- Buyer: `Congratulations — The Keys Are Yours`
- Seller: `Congratulations — It's Official`

## Required Content (in order)

1. **Genuine congratulations** — not generic. Reference something specific about their journey — how long they searched, what made this property the right one, what the sale meant for them (moving on, relocating, estate settled). One to two sentences. Make it personal.

2. **The milestone** — state the close date and the address. Anchor the celebration to the specific thing that happened.

3. **Brief reflection** (optional, agent voice-dependent) — Diana might add a personal note; Sarah might keep it tight and move on. Match the agent's natural style.

4. **Referral ask — natural and earned, not transactional.** This is where the referral relationship begins. Phrase it as a genuine expression of how the agent works, not a sales ask. Something like: "The biggest compliment you can give me is an introduction to someone you know who needs help buying or selling. I'll take care of them the same way I took care of you." The phrasing should be in the agent's voice — it should not sound like a script.

5. **Staying in touch** — one sentence. The agent is there if they have questions, need anything, or just want to talk real estate. Not a hard close — just an open door.

6. **Sign-off** — warm. This is the last line of the active deal.

## Tone Notes
- This is the warmest communication in the system. The relationship does not end at closing — it starts here.
- Personal details matter. A generic congratulations email is forgettable. A specific one lands.
- The referral ask should feel earned and natural, not like a form letter. The agent delivered — now they are asking the client to pay it forward. That is a different ask than a cold solicitation.

## Do Not Include
- Detailed recap of the transaction
- Legal or financial reminders — the deal is done
- Anything that introduces new tasks or concerns for the client
